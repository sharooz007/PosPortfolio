# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Peers and other developers who land on this page to evaluate Sharooz's work. They're technically literate, pattern-match fast, and have seen every dev portfolio trope. They don't need convincing that the tech stack is real — they need to feel that the person behind the work has taste and a point of view, not just skills.

## Product Purpose

A portfolio page that shows Sharooz's real client builds: two POS systems and a set of websites. The page exists to demonstrate that the work is real (for actual paying clients), technically solid, and designed with intent. Success looks like a peer thinking "this person actually ships things" — not "another Tailwind project."

## Brand Personality

Creative, bold, distinctive. A builder with taste. Confident without being loud, personal without being precious.

## Anti-references

- Generic dev portfolio: the dark-bg + neon-green + dot-grid + numbered-sections standard that every bootcamp grad ships. The current page leans too close to this.
- SaaS landing page: feature grids, metric heroes, "built for teams" copy energy.
- Agency/studio aesthetic: overly polished, lots of white space, feels like a firm not a person.

## Design Principles

1. **The work is the hero.** Every design decision should surface the actual builds, not dress them up. If a visual element competes with the project content, cut it.
2. **Earn the distinctiveness.** Creative and bold doesn't mean loud or decorative. It means a specific point of view that couldn't have been generated by reflex.
3. **Say it once, say it well.** No restated headings, no marketing wrappers around plain facts. The copy is already good — protect it from decoration.
4. **Peers see through templates.** Every "clever" dev-portfolio move (dot grids, neon accent, mono labels everywhere, numbered sections) reads as borrowed signal to a technical audience. Distinctiveness comes from restraint in the right places and commitment in the unexpected ones.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. The dark theme must have sufficient contrast — muted text in particular needs to clear 4.5:1 against the background, which the current palette fails.
